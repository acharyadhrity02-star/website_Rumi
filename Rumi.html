Professional Summary

Multidisciplinary researcher specializing in ion-doped bioceramics and electro/magneto-responsive hydrogel systems for bone and soft-tissue regeneration. Experienced in 3D bioprinting (DIW/DLP), nanocomposite design, microneedle platforms, and advanced cellular assays.

Research focus spans controlled ionic microenvironments, antibacterial/antibiofilm scaffolds, and mechanotransduction modulation (e.g., YAP/TAZ, tight junction pathways) using stimulus-enabled bioreactor approaches.

Dissertation Research

Three connected research pillars: ion-doped bioceramics for antibacterial bone scaffolds, dual-ion bioactive glass hydrogels for osteogenesis, and electromagnetic-stimulus hydrogels for soft-tissue regeneration.

Chapter 2

Mg–ND/HAp Composite Scaffolds

Antibacterial bone-regeneration scaffold system integrating Mg²⁺ and nanodiamond with HAp for bioactivity + structure.

  • Controlled Mg²⁺ release (ICP-OES)
  • High antibacterial + antibiofilm activity
  • Enhanced cell migration and cytocompatibility
Chapter 3

ZnO–Mg²⁺/Si Bioactive-Glass Hydrogels

3D-printed hybrid hydrogel designed to improve mechanical performance and osteoblastic differentiation through dual-ion synergy.

  • 3D printing process optimization
  • Rheology + structural evaluation
  • ALP and mineralization enhancement
Chapter 4

Electro-Magnetic Ion-Doped Hydrogels

mCNC-based printable hydrogels enabling EMF-assisted mechanotransduction modulation for soft-tissue regeneration.

  • Fe-ion doped CNC (mCNC) fabrication
  • EMF bioreactor stimulation
  • YAP/TAZ nuclear translocation + cytoskeletal reorganization
